Research:

I currently conduct research under the advisement of Dr. Christine Peterson at MD Anderson. My current project concerns improving the quality of CT scan images taken of cancer patients. Computed Tomography (CT) is often used to diagnose and treat patients with cancer. However, CT scans are not always of ideal quality for radiologists to make accurate judgements for the patient. For example, if there is not enough contrast in the image, then the scan has to be retaken at a later date. This means another trip to the hospital for patients, more time spent per patient for technicians, and an overall strain on hospital resources. I have proposed and am currently constructing a machine learning model that can be used to predict where various venous structures are, so that we may measure its mean pixel value. From this, a decision can be made by technicians on whether or not the image is of good enough quality.
